// 01

Introduction

PitWall Control is the slot racing championship manager: the administrative and season side. It does not time races (that is handled by PitWall, the timing app); Control maintains everything around the competition — drivers, teams, technical catalogues, scrutineering, credits, treasury and standings — and talks to PitWall to send the heats and bring back the results.

  • Runs on desktop (macOS/Windows) and on mobile. The data lives on the device itself.
  • The main navigation, on the left, changes according to the active championship: Home, Drivers, Teams, Events, Scrutineering, Standings, Treasury and Credits.
  • Below the navigation panel you have the secondary shortcuts: Motor draw, Championships, Catalogues, Google settings and CSV templates.
In one sentence

You set up the championship and its catalogues once, and from there you only register, scrutineer and publish: PitWall Control carries the rules for you.

// 02

Create the championship

Everything hangs off a championship. From Championships (secondary shortcut) you create a new one and define how it works. The championship you mark as active is the one you see across the whole app.

Basic details

  • Name, year and organisation (appears in headers and PDFs).
  • Format: Individual (1 driver per team), Pairs (2) or Endurance (teams of up to 6 drivers, typically 4-5).
  • Cups / classes: the list of classes that race (e.g. GRUPO C1, GRUPO C2, GT, CLÁSICOS). They are used for standings and to filter the catalogues during scrutineering.

Technical rules

  • Tooth range allowed in scrutineering: pinion (min/max) and crown gear (min/max). If min = max, the value is fixed (e.g. pinion 12).
  • Organisation motor draw range (min/max numbers). Required in order to draw motors.

Optional modules

  • Credits: enables the handicap system. If you disable it, all the fields and the Credits section are hidden.
  • Treasury: enables fee control per event (and its split). If you disable it, it is hidden.
  • Brand: the championship's own title and tagline for the PDF header and footer. If you leave them empty, the app's default brand is used.
  • Finished: marks the season as closed.
The championship cups are the key piece: almost everything (teams, catalogues, scrutineering, standings) rests on them. Define them before you start registering.
// 03

Catalogues (your library)

The catalogues are the reusable lists of homologated material. They are maintained once and feed the dropdowns during scrutineering. Each tab has a search box and, where relevant, a filter by cup.

  • Cars: model, cup(s) it belongs to and a reference photo (to compare against the car the team hands in). One car can be valid for several cups.
  • Motors: identifier, RPM, gauss and cup(s).
  • Gears: pinions and crown gears (brand and teeth) with cup(s).
  • Tyres: name, reference and cup(s).
  • Motor pods and chassis: with cup(s).
  • Wheels: by axle (front / rear / both), brand and size.
  • Brands, cups and clubs: support lists.
Cup marked vs. unmarked. If an item has no cup marked, it is considered valid for all. If you mark specific cups, it only applies to those. This way, when scrutineering a GRUPO C2 team you will see its material and the "for all" one, but never the one exclusive to GRUPO C1.

You can fill the catalogues by hand, import them via CSV (see CSV templates) or sync them with Google Sheets (see Sync).

// 04

Drivers

The driver is a single master record (name, track record, phone, email) that can take part in several championships. For each championship they have their own profile:

  • Class (PLATINO · ORO · PLATA · BRONCE), the "ladder" used by the season-end bonus and promotion between seasons.
  • Initial/current credits and the previous season's balance (if the championship uses credits).
  • Organising committee: if a driver belongs to the organising committee, their team does not pay a fee.

From Drivers you add, edit and search. To bring in many at once, use the CSV import.

// 05

Teams

A team groups the drivers who share a car and races in a specific cup. The number of drivers depends on the championship format:

  • Individual: 1 driver.
  • Pairs: 2 drivers.
  • Endurance: 1 to 6 drivers (usually 4-5). You add and remove slots in the editor.
The team's cup is what rules in scrutineering: it determines which cars, motors, gears, tyres, motor pods and chassis are offered.

As with drivers, you can create teams by hand or import them via CSV (the endurance template accepts Piloto 1…5 columns).

// 06

Events and heats

An event is each fixture on the calendar (name, venue, date, order). Within the event you set up the heats with their lanes, and you record the teams' entries.

  1. Create the event and order it on the calendar.
  2. Register the participating teams (each entry saves the cup the team races in that event).
  3. Generate the heats and their lane assignment.
The cup is saved as a fixed snapshot at the moment of entry: if the team's cup later changes, the event already played keeps the one it raced.
// 07

Scrutineering

Scrutineering is the administrative heart of race day: for each team you check that its material meets the rules before validating it. You enter from Scrutineering, choose the heat and the team.

Under the team's name the cup it races is shown, and all the dropdowns are filtered by that cup:

  • Car: only models from its cup (or "for all"), with the reference photo below to compare against the car handed in.
  • Motor: organisation (with draw) or own, with RPM/measurements.
  • Pinion and crown gear: brand (restricted to the ones homologated for that cup) and teeth, validated against the championship range.
  • Tyre, motor pod, chassis, wheels: filtered by the cup.
  • Weight, suspension, braid, notes and photos.
If the team's cup has no item marked in the catalogue, the dropdown falls back to the championship cups and, as a last resort, to everything — so you never run out of options, but it always prioritises what belongs to its cup.

Scrutineering autosaves as a draft with every change (top right you see the status: pending / saving / saved), so you can have several open without losing anything. When everything complies, "Everything meets the rules. You can validate" appears and you confirm.

// 08

Standings

The standings are calculated from the event results and the championship rules:

  • Points table per position (defined per championship).
  • Drop scores: number of worst results that do not count.
  • Regularisation cap.
  • By cup: the standings are individual (per driver) within each cup/class.

It is exported to PDF (see Export).

// 09

Treasury

If the championship uses treasury, each event has a fee per team, with a split between organising committee and club. Organising-committee drivers do not pay. From Treasury you keep track of what is collected per event.

// 10

Credits and season close

The credits system is the championship's handicap: each driver starts with a number of credits and these go up or down depending on the result. From Credits you see the balance, the movements (each with its description) and you manage the season close.

Season-end bonus

At the end of the season a bonus is applied based on the driver's class and the number of races contested (bands configurable per championship). The movement is described (e.g. "bonificación por 6 carreras").

Class review

Before finishing, the Class review screen lets you decide, driver by driver, whether they are promoted, relegated or stay (Up / Down / Keep buttons over the BRONCE→PLATA→ORO→PLATINO ladder). That resulting class is used for the bonus and as the initial class when importing into the next championship. The screen has a search by name.

Adjust the classes first in Review and then apply the season-end bonus: that way both use the same final snapshot.
// 11

Motor draw

For organisation motors, PitWall Control draws a number within the range configured in the championship, deducting the ones already assigned in the event. You can draw from the Motor draw shortcut (choosing the event) or directly inside the team's scrutineering.

// 12

Sync with Google Sheets

The catalogues can be synced two-way with a Google Sheet, handy for editing as a team or making a copy. From Google settings you connect your account and point to the sheet.

  1. Connect your Google account and authorise access.
  2. Point to the spreadsheet you will use as the catalogue.
  3. Pull from the Sheet brings the data into the app; Push to the Sheet sends yours. Conflicts are resolved row by row.
Setting up the Google connection (credentials and permissions) is explained in the configuration manual. Here only usage is covered.
// 13

CSV templates

From CSV templates (secondary, global shortcut) you download the templates ready to fill in and import: drivers, teams (including the endurance one with Equipo · Copa · Piloto 1…5 columns) and catalogues. You fill in the CSV, import it from the corresponding section and the app maps the columns automatically.

// 14

Export to PDF

PitWall Control generates PDFs of scrutineering, heats, standings and credits. When exporting you choose the document language (Spanish, English, Italian or French) and the championship brand (title and tagline) is applied to the header and footer.

The heat PDF fits on a single sheet (A4 → A3 if needed) so it prints comfortably on the scrutineers' table.
// 15

Send to PitWall / bring back results

PitWall Control talks to the PitWall timing app so you don't type things twice:

  • Export / send the heat: generates the heat (pitwall.tanda/v1 format) and sends it over LAN to PitWall, or saves it as JSON. The rest slots (D1…Dk) are handled correctly.
  • Bring back the results: imports the event results from PitWall over the local network, ready for standings and credits.
// 16

Glossary

  • Championship: the container for the whole season (format, cups, rules, modules).
  • Cup / class: the class in which a team races (GRUPO C1, GT, Clásicos…). It filters catalogues and groups the standings.
  • Format: Individual (1), Pairs (2) or Endurance (up to 6 drivers per team).
  • Catalogue: reusable library of material (cars, motors, gears, tyres, motor pods, chassis, wheels…).
  • Driver profile: the driver's data in a championship (class, credits).
  • Event: each fixture on the calendar; contains heats and entries.
  • Heat: one run of the event with its lane assignment.
  • Scrutineering: checking a team's material against the rules before validating it.
  • Reference photo: image of the car in the catalogue to compare against the one the team hands in.
  • Credits: handicap system; they go up/down depending on the result.
  • Season-end bonus: credits added at the end based on class and number of races.
  • Class review: promotion/relegation of each driver before closing (BRONCE→PLATINO ladder).
  • Class ladder: BRONCE · PLATA · ORO · PLATINO.
  • Drop score: worst result that does not count towards the standings.
  • Treasury: fees per event and their split (organising committee / club).
  • Heat: the event package (pitwall.tanda/v1) sent to PitWall.
  • Brand: the championship's title and tagline stamped onto the PDFs.
